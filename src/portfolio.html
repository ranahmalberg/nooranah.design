Improving reading and publishing experiences for writers with Vocal
User research • Exploratory (Survey → Inteviews) • Product strategy

Context

Vocal is a publishing platform that allows writers to monetise their posts based on reads and win prizes from being a top entry in writing challenges. In 2022, Vocal approached Thinkmill to help grow their readership and improve the writer experience after the last success of shipping the product to market.

For the wider context of the long-term engagement, this is an overview from Thinkmill:

We helped Vocal scale from a hunch to a multi-million user platform.

Learn more on Thinkmill

Bottoms up problem-solving

Since the users were writers, the Vocal team had really thoughtful, well-written feedback that creators posted and tagged as 'reviews'. I spent about 2 days reading and theming up these posts. We found that the main downsides to the platform users shared were either about an inefficient story editor experience or how difficult it was to grow an readership/audience compared to other publishing platforms.

This feedback gave us a good sense of pain-points to validate when we talk to a more representative sample of users.

Research plan

We decided on a mixed methods approach with a quantitative survey to first understand the different thinking styles of users on the platform, how they currently use it and their attitude towards Vocal.

Based on the survey results, we recruited participants for remote focus groups to cover and capture thoughts across a range of user motivations and attitudes.

This survey → interview approach worked well because our research questions were exploratory: Who are reading or posting on the platform and why? What does support mean to a writer on Vocal? What are the current challenges writers are facing on the platform? Responses from the survey laid the groundwork for deeper exploration in 1:1s.
